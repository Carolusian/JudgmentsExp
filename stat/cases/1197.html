WONG SHING HUNG v. THE HONG KONG AND CHINA GAS CO LTD AND ANOTHER [1974] HKCFI 97; HCA2599/1973 (20 May 1974)

HCA002599/1973

1973, No. 2599

IN THE SUPREME COURT OF HONG KONG

ORIGINAL JURISDICTION

-----------------

BETWEEN
WONG SHING HUNG Administrator of the Estate of WONG WAI SAU Deceased Plaintiff
and
THE HONG KONG AND CHINA GAS COMPANY LIMITED Defendant
and
THE ATTORNEY GENERAL Third Party

-----------------

Coram: Mr. Registrar Jones in Chambers.

Date of Judgment: 20th May. 1974.

-----------------

DECISION

-----------------

1. The plaintiff in this action claims damages against the defendant in respect of the death of his daughter who was killed on the 14thOctober 1972 as a result of a gas explosion that occurred at the Daimaru Department Store where the deceased was an employee.

2. A third party notice claiming an indemnity or a contribution was issued by the defendant against the Attorney General alleging thatthe deceased's death was caused because of the negligence of the Fire Services Department. An unconditional appearance was enteredby the third party although it would appear having regard to the nature of the present application to have been appropriate to filea conditional appearance. A statement of claim has been filed by the defendant which the third party by this summons applies to strikeout on the ground that it discloses nor reasonable cause of action in that as the statement of claim contains no allegation of lackof bona fides in the exercise of powers, by virtue of the provisions of sections 4 and 27 of the Crown Proceedings Ordinance Cap. 300 the Third Party is entitled to the protection afforded by section 22 of the Fire Services Ordinance Cap. 95. Section 4 of the Crown Proceedings Ordinance deals with the liability of the Crown in tort and section 27 deals with the application to the Crown of certain statutory provisions.

3. Mr. Evans who appeared for the Attorney General argued that even if negligence is established by the defendant section 22 of the Fire Services Ordinance provides the Crown with an absolute right to immunity. Section 22 provides that:-

'No member acting bona fide under powers conferred by this Ordinance shall be liable to any action for damages for any act done oromitted to be done by him in connexion with his duties on the occasion of a fire or other calamity.'

4. In his submission Mr. Evans cited a New Zealand case Tally v. Motueka Borough(1) and a decision of the High Court of Australia Board of Fire Commissioners of New South Wales v. Arbouin(2) in which cases the question of immunity was claimed under provisions similar to section 22 which lends weight to his argument although much of what was said was obiter. However, it appears that the matter has not so farbeen argued in Hong Kong.

5. Mr. Jackson-Lipkin for the defendant submitted that the issue raised by the third party is a pleading point and is analogous to acase where the Limitation Ordinance is pleaded in an action which is statute barred. Mr. Evans contended that defendant should have specifically pleaded mala fides,but I do not agree with this proposition. It may of course be necessary to do so when the defendant replies to the defence.

6. I am of the opinion that the matter raised by the third party is one that is properly to be determined by the trial judge upon theevidence with the result that I accept Mr. Jackson-Lipkin's submission that if the third party wishes to invoke immunity it shouldbe pleaded in their defence. In the circumstances it is unnecessary for me to deal with the other points that were raised duringthe course of the argument.

7. Accordingly the summons will be dismissed with costs to the defendant in the third party proceedings. There will be a certificatefor counsel.

8. Dated this the 20th day of May, 1974.

(B.L. Jones)

Acting Deputy Registrar
