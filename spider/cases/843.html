HCMP 96/2000

IN THE HIGH COURT OF THE

HONG KONG SPECIAL ADMINISTRATIVE REGION

COURT OF APPEAL

MISCELLANEOUS PROCEEDINGS NO. 96 OF 2000

(ON APPEAL FROM: HCA 5771 OF 1999)

------------------------------------------

BETWEEN

CHAU Chiu-yee

Plaintiff

and

CHEUNG Lung

LAI Kwong-yung

------------------------------------------

Defendants

Coram: Hon Chan CJHC and K.H. Woo JA in Court

Date of Hearing: 2nd June 2000

Date of Judgment:2nd June 2000

------------------------------------

JUDGMENT

-------------------------------------

Chan, CJHC (delivering the judgment of the Court):

1. On 8th April 1999, the Applicant brought an action to the High Court against Cheung Lung and Lai Kwong Yung for damages. The Applicant had applied to the Court several times demanding the Defendants to attend court but he did not know the address of the Defendants. On 29th June 1999, Master Poon struck out the action in this matter on the basis that the cause of action in this case has exceeded the statutory time limit.

2. On 25th August 1999, the Applicant lodged an appeal to the Court of First Instance of the High Court. The appeal was called on 9th September 1999 but the Applicant was late. Judge Chung adjourned the matter to 21 October 1999 and reminded the Applicant that heshould be punctual on that occasion. However the Applicant was absent on 21 October 1999. Judge Chung therefore dismissed the appeal with reasons listed out in his judgement. Subsequently the Applicant applied afresh to Judge Chung asking the Judge towithdraw his order for the dismissal of the appeal. On 9th December 1999, before Judge Chung he offered an explanation for his absence but that was not accepted by the Judge who then refusedhis application.

3. On 7th January 2000, the Applicant brought an application to the Court of Appeal asking the Court to allow his appeal against the judgementof Judge Chung. However M Wong JA refused his application. The Applicant now applies afresh to this Court.

4. The entirety of the statement of claim of the Applicant is as follows:

5. “The Claimant Chau Chiu Yee, trading as Yee Kee Construction and Decoration Engineering Company, suspected that on 8th December 1987, Cheung Lung conspired with Mr. Lai Kwong Yung, who posed as the landlord, and presumptuously asked two male subordinatesto take away my tools and materials. As a result, I could not take up any works. It caused me a lot of damages. Therefore I demanded that they should pay a sum of two hundred thousand as damages.”

6. This Court is of the view that on the presumption that the claim of the Applicant is valid, the cause of action of this case is a tortious act which occurred in 1987, and yet the Applicant did not take the action to court until 1999 which was some 11 years after the occurrence.

7. Pursuant to section 4(1)(a) of the Limitation Ordinance Cap 347, an action of tort shall not be brought after the expiration of 6 years from the date on which the cause of action accrued. Even if the claim of the Applicant is valid, when he brought the action in 1999, the prescribed time limit had expired.Therefore no action could be brought on that cause of action.

8. The Applicant explained that he could not bring the action any earlier because he had applied to the Legal Aid Department for legalaid many times, but his application was refused. However, Judge Chung was of the view that the explanation could not stand, and thesame view is shared by this Court. The Applicant could have brought the action at any time during those 11 years, just as what hedid on 8th April 1999. Should this matter continue, the Applicant has no chance of success at all. Thus Master Poon was right to dismiss his case and Judge Chung was correct to dismiss his appeal. This Court refuses the Applicants’ application to appeal.
(P Chan) (K.H. Woo)
Chief Judge of the High Court Justice of Appeal

The Plaintiff: Chau Chiu-yee, acting in person
