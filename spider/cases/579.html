JUDGMENT

-------------------------------------

1. The Applicant is the Plaintiff in this matter. The Applicant brought an action to the High Court on 8th April 1999. In his statement of claim, he claimed he suspected that on 8th December 1987, the two defendants conspired with the landlord to presumptuously take away the tools and materials that belonged to the Plaintiff. Therefore, the Plaintiff demanded that the Defendants should pay him $200,000of damages.

2. According to the statement of claim, what the Defendants did on 8th December 1987 constituted some ‘tortious acts’ and those acts occurred about 11 years before this action was brought.

3. Section 4(1)(a) of the Limitation Ordinance Cap 347 sets out that:-

“The following actions shall not be brought after the expiration of 6 years from the date on which the cause of action accrued, that is to say-

(a) actions founded on simple contract or on tort; ...”

4. During the period between 5th and 25th May 1999, the Applicant made 4 basically identical applications to the Court requesting the Defendants to attend Court. The Master heard the matter on 22nd June 1999 and struck it out. The Applicant was not satisfied with the decision and made this application on 25th August 1999 for leave to appeal out of time.

5. The Applicant failed to give a reasonable explanation as to why he could not lodge the appeal within the time limit. His ‘explanation’is that he applied for legal aid on 22nd June 1999 and he felt that he had to wait for the decision of the Legal Aid Department before he could proceed with the appeal.

6. Besides, this Court is of the view that section 4(1)(a) of the Limitation Ordinance is applicable to the cause of action put forward by the Plaintiff in this case. Consequently, the tortious acts involved in the case had gone beyond the 6-year limitation prescribed by the Ordinance. Furthermore, this Court thinks that thereis not any evidence or reasonable ground for the Court to rely on section 22 of the Limitation Ordinance to extend the limitation period. Therefore, the Court exercises its inherent jurisdiction to strike out the case so that any waste on public resources and time isavoided.

7. In view of the above reasons, the Applicant has not shown that he will stand a chance of success at the appeal. The Court thereforedismisses this application.

8. One more point is that the first hearing of this application was on 9th September 1999. The Applicant was late on that occasion and this Court reminded him that he should be absolutely punctual when heattended court this time (21st October 1999). The Applicant was not present even at 10 a.m. this morning. Having considered all the circumstances, this Court comesto the conclusion that the application of the Applicant should be dismissed as he is absent, if not for the above reasons.

(Andrew Chung)

Judge of the Court of First Instance
High Court

The Plaintiff, absent and unrepresented.

The 1st Defendant and 2nd Defendant, absent and unrepresented.
