J U D G M E N T

__________________

Hon Rogers VP:

1. We have considered the submissions made by counsel both for and against leave to appeal. It appears to us that the legal point involvedin this case is by no means an easy one. It is complex and, indeed, the authority in relation to vicarious liability for torts committedby a third party is sparse. The authority that we have relied upon in our judgments is an authority of the highest court in Australiabut does not appear to have been cited in other jurisdictions.

2. We consider that the matter involved, as I have indicated, of vicarious liability for torts committed by agents, is a matter of greatgeneral or public importance and, in those circumstances, this Court is disposed to grant leave subject to conditions.

(Anthony Rogers) (Brian Keith) (Doreen Le Pichon)
